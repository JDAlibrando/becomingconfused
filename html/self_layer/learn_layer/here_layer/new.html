People float down the river,
familiar with the water that accompanies them.

So do I until I brace against a stone
and feel the water push past my petite frame,
my feet tingling with the depth of the earth.

The water that once held me,
I realize, had another agenda,
to continue down its trajectory,
as eager to leave me behind
as to insist on my return
to the rivers flow.

So, I am left behind
by that force that once moved me
or tossed me in its turbulence.

And, I wonder
what to do now.
