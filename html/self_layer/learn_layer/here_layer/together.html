  • Knowing a Juniper Tree

Stand beside me,
in the space that separates us,
and share the sight of this juniper tree.

When we share this juniper,
do our worlds not overlap?
Does its form—
the unity of the storm-blown trunk and crystal blue-green scales,
that evades our grasp as flames through our fingers,
and projects dancing shade onto all eyes in its purview
—give us a glimpse of each other?

When we share the image
of this juniper,
its stormy trunk and crystalline scales,
do we not share the moment as mirrors?
You, me, and tree reflecting each,
as a transparent flame
in a hall of mirrors.

If I am to peer past our multiverse,
and understand this tree as singular
not the multitudes of eye-cast storms and crystals,
but as the hidden flame that projects becoming to them.

Then
maybe I will again remember why
we can know the Other,
as we share the sight of this juniper tree.
