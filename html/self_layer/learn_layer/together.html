  • Knowing a Juniper Tree

Stand beside me,
in the space that separates us,
and share the sight of this juniper tree.

When we share this juniper
do our worlds not overlap?!
Does its unity—
the gnarled trunk and scale-like leaves,
that evades our understanding like flames through our fingers
and projects dancing shade onto all eyes in its purview—
give us a glimpse of each other?

When we share the image of this juniper,
its storm and scales,
do we not share the moment as mirrors?!
You, me, and tree reflecting each.
A transparent flame lost in a hall of mirrors.

It is obvious and impossible
that space is so impenetrable
but for shadows on the wall of a pinhole camera.
Doubt is persistent, but…

If I am to peer behind my eyes
and understand this juniper as singular,
not as the multitudes of eye-cast shadows
but as the lost flame that projects becoming to them, then…

Maybe I will again remember why
we can know the Other
as we share the sight of this juniper tree.
